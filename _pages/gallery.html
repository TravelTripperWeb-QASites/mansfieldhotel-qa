Built in 1903, as a single gentlemen's hotel on Manhattan's Club Row, The Mansfield is a historic hotel designed in the Beaux Arts style by James Renwick, the famed architect behind St. Patrick's Cathedral and the New York Public Library. Although updated, The Mansfield preserves much of its original style and old world charm, offering guests a rare glimpse into a bygone era.
